Part 2

Dividends and ROI

Returns are not just price changes.

Worked Example

Preferred shareholders get paid first

1

Preferred dividend/share: 8% × $50 = $4.00

2

Total preferred: $4.00 × 1,000 = $4,000

3

Remainder: $10,000 − $4,000 = $6,000

4

Common dividend/share: $6,000 ÷ 4,000 = $1.50

=(TotalDiv - (Rate * Par * PreferredShares)) / CommonSharesUse inputs, not typed answer numbers.

Worked Example

A bond quote is a percent of par

Worked Example

NAV prices one share of the fund

=(Holdings - Liabilities) / Shares=($5,550,000 - $770,000) / 600,000NAV = $7.97/share
=NAV * SharesToBuy=$7.97 * 80Purchase cost = $637.60

What the number means

One mutual fund share represents a claim on the fund's net assets after liabilities.

Common Mistake

Do not treat a bond quote as dollars

Incorrect

A quote of 99.59 means the bond costs $99.59.

Correct

A quote of 99.59 means 99.59% of $1,000, or $995.90.

That conversion changes every downstream answer: total cost, current yield, and premium/discount classification.
