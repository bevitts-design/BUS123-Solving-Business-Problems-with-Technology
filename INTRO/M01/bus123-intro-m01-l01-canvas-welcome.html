A note from Prof. Evitts

Start with confidence. This is a class about becoming capable, not proving you already are.

If this is your first college class, it is normal for everything to feel new at once: Canvas, deadlines, files, Excel, and even the rhythm of college work. We will take it one step at a time.

The students who do well are not always the students who start with the most experience. They are the students who build steady habits, ask questions early, and keep their files organized enough to trust their own work.

What this class builds

Digital confidence, spreadsheet fluency, business judgment, and professional file habits.

First week

Do these 3 things first.

01

Read the syllabus. You can do this now.

Start with expectations, grading, communication, and the basic rhythm of the course.

02

Sign into Microsoft 365.

Use your Endicott account so Excel, Word, PowerPoint, and OneDrive connect to the right place. We will do this together in class.

03

Create your BUS123 OneDrive folder.

Save course work where it syncs, backs up, and stays easy to find when deadlines arrive. Again, we will do this together in class.

Tools + decisions

Technology matters when it helps someone make a better decision.

In BUS123, Excel is not just a grid of cells. It is where business questions become models, checks, charts, and recommendations.

AI can help you move faster. Your job is to understand the numbers, check the formulas, and decide whether the output makes sense for the business situation.

You will practice
  • Organizing files so work does not disappear.
  • Building Excel workbooks that update correctly.
  • Using business math to support decisions.
  • Reading outputs before trusting them.
  • Explaining what the numbers mean.
Habits that win

Small habits make the class feel much easier.

Save in OneDrive Make the cloud folder your default.
Name files clearly Your file name should identify the work.
Check before submitting Open the file and review the output.
Ask early Questions are easier before the deadline.
Practice week by week Confidence comes from repetition.
Support

You are not expected to figure everything out alone.

Office hours and help details are posted in Canvas. Use them early, especially if Excel feels unfamiliar or a file is not saving where you expect.

Good question to ask: "Can you help me check whether I set this up correctly?" That is exactly the kind of habit this class is meant to build.

One sentence to remember

You do not need to be perfect on day one. You need a system, steady practice, and the willingness to check your work.
