What is a
business problem?

10 minutes
Group Activity
Get into groups of three. What is a business problem?
Pick a real business you use or know.
Name a specific problem they had to solve — not vague.
Be ready to share one sentence: the company, the problem, and what they had to figure out.
Go deeper

Most business problems fit one of three buckets.

📊
Information
We don't have the right data.
Decisions get made without knowing what's actually happening. Better data means better choices.
🔗
Coordination
The right hand doesn't know what the left is doing.
Teams, departments, and vendors all pulling in different directions without a shared system.
⚡
Efficiency
Something costs too much or takes too long.
A process that should take an hour takes a week. The right tool cuts it down.
The scale
90%
of business tasks involve creating, analyzing, or sharing information — and most of those run through Excel, Word, or PowerPoint. Fluency isn't optional.
Watch out
⚠ Common Mistake
Saving files to Desktop — and losing them when your laptop fails.
❌ What students do
Save everything to Desktop or Downloads. Laptop breaks or gets wiped. Semester's work is gone.
✅ The rule
If it's not in OneDrive, it doesn't exist. Every file for this course lives in your BUS123 OneDrive folder. Get in the habit today.
