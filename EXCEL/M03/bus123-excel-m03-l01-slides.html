Warm-Up: The Price Tag Problem

02

Tidal Goods Co. buys a reusable bottle for $8.40 and sells it for $14.99.

$6.59dollars above cost
78.5%markup on cost
44.0%gross margin of price

All three numbers describe the same sale, but they answer different business questions.

Current Retail Question

03

Managers see margin

Concept Foundation

Cost, Price, Markup, Margin

Same transaction, different lenses.

Cost vs. Selling Price

06

Unit cost

What the company pays to acquire or produce one item before it is sold.

Selling price

What the customer pays at checkout before sales tax, returns, or later discounts.

=Selling_Price - Unit_Cost

Manual check: $14.99 - $8.40 = $6.59

Dollar Markup

07
=Price - Cost

The dollar amount added above the item cost.

ItemCostPriceDollar Markup
Reusable bottle$8.40$14.99
Expected result$6.59

Markup Rate

08
=Dollar_Markup / Cost

Manual check: $6.59 / $8.40 = 78.5%

Question answered

How large is the markup compared with what the store paid?

Markup rate uses cost as the denominator.

Gross Margin Percent

09
=Dollar_Markup / Selling_Price

Manual check: $6.59 / $14.99 = 44.0%

Question answered

What share of the selling price remains after covering the item cost?

Margin percent uses selling price as the denominator.

Common Mistake: Markup vs. Margin

10

Markup rate and margin percent are not interchangeable.

=Dollar_Markup / Cost

Markup compares profit dollars to cost.

=Dollar_Markup / Selling_Price

Margin compares profit dollars to price.

Excel Walkthrough

Syntax First, Math Second

Excel is the primary tool. Manual math verifies the result.

Step 1: Dollar Markup

16
=E5-D5

Cell E5 holds the current selling price. Cell D5 holds the unit cost.

Fill down

After the first row works, copy the formula down for every product. Relative references update by row.

Step 2: Markup Rate

17
=G5/D5

Dollar markup divided by cost.

Format as Percent

The formula returns a decimal. Excel percent formatting makes 0.785 display as 78.5%.

Step 3: Gross Margin Percent

18
=G5/E5

Dollar markup divided by selling price.

Business reading

A 44.0% margin means 44 cents of each sales dollar remains after the product cost.

Step 4: Markdown Price

19
=E5*(1-F5)

Current price multiplied by the percentage kept after discount.

=E5-J5

Current price minus markdown price gives the dollar markdown loss.

Common Mistake: Rounding Too Early

21

Do not type rounded intermediate answers into later formulas.

Risk

A rounded markup rate can make margin and markdown decisions drift from the actual dollars.

Better habit

Reference cells and format the display. Let Excel keep the underlying precision.
