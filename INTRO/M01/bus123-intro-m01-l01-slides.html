Setting Up OneDrive

Your work needs to be accessible from any device, any time. OneDrive makes that automatic.

What Is OneDrive?

07

OneDrive is Microsoft's cloud storage and sync service. Files saved to OneDrive are:

  • Stored in the cloud — not just on your local hard drive
  • Synced automatically — changes on one device appear on all others
  • Accessible anywhere — browser, app, phone, tablet
  • Backed up — if your laptop is stolen or dies, your work is safe
  • Shareable — send a link instead of an email attachment
🏫

Your Endicott account includes 1 TB of OneDrive storage — far more than you will ever need for coursework.

☁️ → 💻
One file. Every device.

Edit on your laptop at the library. Review on your phone at lunch. Present from a classroom computer. Same file. Always current.

Common Mistake

11
⚠️
Saving to Personal OneDrive Instead of Endicott OneDrive

Many students have a personal Microsoft account (Hotmail, Outlook.com). When OneDrive opens, it may default to that personal account — not your Endicott account.

  • How to spot it: In File Explorer, look at your sidebar. You should see two OneDrive entries: "Personal" and "Endicott College." If you only see one, you may not be signed in to your school account.
  • Why it matters: Personal OneDrive storage may be limited, and your files won't be accessible with school credentials if you reset your password.
  • Fix: Open OneDrive Settings → Account → Add an account → sign in with your @endicott.edu address.

How Sync Works — The Short Version

13

When you save a file to your Endicott OneDrive folder, the sync process runs in three stages:

🔵
Uploading — the file is being sent to the cloud. Do not close your laptop or lose internet during this stage.
✅
Synced — the file is safely stored in the cloud and identical to your local copy. Safe to close the app.
⚠️
Error / Paused — check your internet connection. The file is still local but has not uploaded yet.
Rule of Thumb
Always wait for the green checkmark before submitting

"I saved it" and "it synced to OneDrive" are two different things. When submitting work in Canvas, verify the file is fully synced — not still uploading — before copying the share link.

File Naming Convention

Professional file management starts with a name your future self can find.

File Naming in BUS123

15

All files you submit in this course follow a standard naming convention. This is a professional skill — employers and colleagues rely on it.

Required Format
LastName_FirstName_BUS123_ModuleN.xlsx
Example: Smith_Jordan_BUS123_Module01.xlsx
  • No spaces — use underscores between segments
  • Last name first — allows alphabetical sorting in the gradebook
  • Module number — zero-padded (Module01, not Module1)
  • Extension matches the app — .xlsx for Excel, .docx for Word, .pdf for PDFs
  • Never: "HW1", "final", "DONE", "v2", or your first name only

Common Mistake

17
⚠️
Using the Browser Version of Excel Instead of the Desktop App

Excel Online (the browser version at office.com) looks similar but is missing hundreds of features we will use this semester. It is not the same tool.

  • Missing features: Many advanced formulas, formatting options, data analysis tools, and the full function library are not available in the browser version.
  • How to tell: Desktop Excel has a thick green ribbon and a "File" tab that opens to a backstage view. Browser Excel has a simplified ribbon and says "Excel" in a browser tab.
  • Fix: Always launch Excel from your Applications or Start menu. Right-click a OneDrive file → Open in App — never let it open in the browser by default.
