Staffing Shift Puzzle

Assign limited weekly staff blocks across the clinic schedule. The objective is to lower the average wait time and make waits more consistent.

Operations rule

Select a staff member, then click a day cell to assign one available shift block. Staff can work multiple days, but each person has a finite number of blocks for the week and each day can hold up to three added blocks.

Objective: Hit both targets: average projected wait time at or below 15.5 minutes and sample standard deviation at or below 4.0 minutes.
Role context: RNs handle clinical assessment, triage, medication questions, and patient escalation. MAs room patients, take vitals, prep charts, and keep visits moving. The Float can cover either role, but should be saved for bottleneck days.
Hours context: Full-time clinical staff usually work 36-40 hours across the week. Adding coverage to a day means shifting available hours, not creating unlimited labor.
Start by covering Monday and Friday, the highest-volume days.

Role guide

RN blocks reduce clinical bottlenecks most on high-acuity days. MA blocks reduce rooming and prep delays on steady clinic days. Float is flexible coverage for the week's biggest mismatch.

Targets

Success means average wait <= 15.5 minutes and standard deviation <= 4.0 minutes. A low average with a high standard deviation still means some days are painful for patients.
