# AI-Assisted Investment Model Audit

## Business Scenario

Meridian Advisory Group is preparing a short investment recommendation for a client who is considering whether to buy shares of **Evergreen Fitness Technologies (EFT)**, a fictional public company that sells connected fitness equipment and subscription software.

An analyst used AI to help assemble an Excel workbook. The workbook looks useful at first glance, but it contains calculation errors, formatting problems, weak chart choices, and AI-generated claims that need to be verified.

Your role is not to blindly accept or reject AI. Your role is to use AI like a junior assistant: ask for help, check the work, repair the model, and explain what you trusted, changed, or rejected.

> Can you turn a flawed AI-assisted investment workbook into a reliable client recommendation?

## What You Receive

You will receive a flawed starter workbook with:

1. An `AI Draft Notes` tab containing AI-generated advice.
2. A `Stock Snapshot` tab with stock quote and dividend data.
3. A `Investment Return` tab with ROI calculations.
4. A `Bond Check` tab with bond quote calculations.
5. A `Fund NAV` tab with mutual fund net asset value calculations.
6. A `Financial Statements` tab with income statement, balance sheet, and ratio information.
7. A `Dashboard` tab with a chart and recommendation area.
8. A `Prompt Log` tab where you document your AI use.

The workbook is intentionally flawed. Some errors are obvious. Some are subtle. Some AI suggestions sound confident but are financially wrong.

## Learning Goal

This capstone is designed to prove that AI does not replace the skills from BUS123. AI can help you work faster, but you still need Excel, business math, formatting judgment, and finance vocabulary to know whether the answer is correct.

You should use skills from:

- Excel M01-M04: formulas, references, formatting, functions, charts, and data visualization.
- Math M10: stock quotes, bid-ask spread, dividend yield, ROI, bond quote conversion, current yield, and NAV.
- Math M11: income statement logic, balance sheet logic, ratio analysis, trend analysis, and business interpretation.

## AI Use Rules

You may use approved AI tools for:

- Diagnosing possible workbook errors.
- Suggesting formulas or formatting improvements.
- Explaining finance terms.
- Helping draft a plain-English recommendation.

You may not submit AI output without checking it. AI suggestions are evidence to evaluate, not answers to paste.

Required AI evidence:

1. Record **5-8 meaningful prompts** in the `Prompt Log`.
2. For each major accepted fix, explain:
   - What AI suggested.
   - What course skill, formula, or source you used to verify it.
   - What you changed in the workbook.
3. Include at least **two AI suggestions you rejected or revised**.
4. Be ready to defend one formula fix, one formatting fix, one chart/dashboard choice, and one AI suggestion you did not fully trust.

## Required Work

### 1. Audit Before AI

Before asking AI for help, review the workbook yourself. On the `Prompt Log` tab, write at least five issues you notice during your own audit.

Look for:

- Formula errors or formulas that answer the wrong question.
- Hardcoded calculated values.
- Percent values shown in the wrong format.
- Currency values shown without currency formatting.
- Labels that do not explain the unit or business meaning.
- Charts that look polished but do not answer the client question.
- AI-written statements that overclaim or ignore risk.

### 2. Use AI as a Diagnostic Assistant

Use AI to help you inspect the workbook. Good prompts ask AI to reason through a specific part of the model instead of asking for the final answer.

Example prompt starters:

- "Review this ROI calculation. What inputs should be included, and what mistake might occur if dividends are ignored?"
- "What is the correct way to convert a bond quote of 98.75 into a dollar price?"
- "What should I check before trusting a P/E ratio interpretation?"
- "How can I make this dashboard easier for a client to read without changing the underlying numbers?"

### 3. Repair the Workbook

Correct the workbook so that it becomes a reliable investment analysis package.

Your repaired workbook should include:

- Correct formulas for stock return, dividend yield, bond dollar price, current yield, NAV, income statement subtotals, and financial ratios.
- Professional number formatting for currency, percentages, multiples, and counts.
- Clear labels and units.
- A cleaner dashboard that supports the recommendation.
- A recommendation that acknowledges uncertainty and risk.

Do not delete the `Prompt Log`. It is part of your submission.

### 4. Improve the Dashboard

The starter dashboard is intentionally weak. Improve it so that a client can quickly see:

- Whether the stock return calculation is positive or negative.
- Whether key ratios support or weaken the recommendation.
- At least one useful chart or visual comparison.
- A short recommendation with evidence.

Your chart should answer a business question. A chart that merely looks attractive but misrepresents the data should be fixed.

### 5. Write the Client Recommendation

Write a short recommendation in the workbook that answers:

> Should the client buy, watch, or avoid EFT based on the repaired model?

Your recommendation must:

- Use evidence from the corrected workbook.
- Mention at least one risk or limitation.
- Explain how AI helped and where your own judgment mattered.

## Deliverables

Submit:

1. Corrected Excel workbook.
2. Completed `Prompt Log` tab.
3. Short client recommendation in the workbook.
4. Be prepared for a 2-3 minute defense.

## Defense Questions

Your instructor may ask:

- Which formula error mattered most and why?
- Which AI suggestion did you reject or revise?
- How did you verify the corrected ROI, bond, NAV, or ratio formula?
- What formatting change made the workbook more trustworthy?
- Why is your chart better than the original dashboard chart?
- What would you tell a client about the limits of this analysis?
