What Is BUS123?

05

BUS123 is Solving Business Problems with Technology — a practical, skill-based course where you learn to use industry-standard software to analyze, communicate, and solve real business challenges.

No Textbook. No SIMnet.

All materials are built for this course — slide decks, Excel workbooks, math modules, and practice files. Everything lives on Canvas and GitHub. You'll learn by doing, not by reading.

💼

Every exercise in this course is grounded in real business scenarios. The formulas you write and the reports you build reflect what professionals actually do at work.

Three Tracks

  • EXCEL — Worksheets, formulas, functions, charts, data management
  • MATH — Business math through applied Excel problems: percents, discounts, payroll, markup, inventory, finance, taxes, and more
Class Format

Our Three Case Study Businesses

07

Every exercise and homework problem in BUS123 is set inside one of these three businesses. Get to know them — they'll be recurring characters all semester.

🏄
Harbor View Surf Shop
A specialty retail shop selling surfboards, wetsuits, apparel, and accessories. Operates seasonally with significant inventory, payroll, and sales tracking needs. Great for retail math, payroll, and cost analysis problems.
👗
Dune Road Apparel
A small clothing brand that designs and sells coastal-inspired apparel. Uses trade and cash discounts, manages invoices, and tracks product markup and markdown. Great for commercial math and discount problems.
🏢
Endicott Management Group
A consulting and portfolio management firm — abbreviated EMG. Handles financial analysis, tax planning, investment tracking, and reporting for clients. Great for finance math, TVM, and data analysis problems.

Why Case Studies?

08

Abstract math is easy to forget. Problems with context and narrative are much easier to learn — and much more like what you'll face in a real job.

  • You'll build payroll tables for Harbor View's employees — not just "calculate gross pay"
  • You'll apply trade discounts for Dune Road's wholesale orders — not just "use this formula"
  • You'll prepare investment summaries for EMG clients — not just "compute present value"
What This Looks Like in Practice

Harbor View Surf Shop needs to calculate gross pay for 6 employees. Some are hourly, some are salaried. Two worked overtime last week.

Your job: build a payroll table in Excel that calculates gross pay, deductions, and net pay automatically — and formats it so the store owner can read it.

🎯

That is a real deliverable. Not a textbook exercise — a professional-quality file that could be handed to an actual employer.

Common Mistake

12
⚠️
Submitting the Wrong File — or a Corrupted One

The most common submission error is uploading the wrong version of a file, or a file that won't open because it wasn't saved properly. Instructors cannot grade a file they can't open.

  • Always open the submitted file after uploading to Canvas — download it back and verify it opens correctly and shows your work.
  • Never submit a file that's currently open in Excel — always save and close before uploading. Some browsers will upload a locked temp version.
  • Check the file name before submitting. If it doesn't match the required naming convention, rename it before you upload — not after.
  • Sync first: if the file lives in OneDrive and you're submitting a link, confirm the green checkmark is showing before copying the URL.

Common Mistake

17
⚠️
Waiting Until the Night Before to Figure Out the Tech

In every semester, some students skip the setup steps in Week 1 planning to do them later — and then hit a crisis at 11 PM before a homework deadline.

  • OneDrive not syncing at midnight before a due date is not an acceptable reason for a late submission. Set it up now, in class, where help is available.
  • Microsoft 365 activation issues take time to resolve with IT. If you run into a problem, contact Endicott IT support immediately — not the night before the first assignment is due.
  • Technical problems must be reported before the deadline. "My computer crashed" submitted after the fact is not the same as "I had a problem and I contacted you immediately."
