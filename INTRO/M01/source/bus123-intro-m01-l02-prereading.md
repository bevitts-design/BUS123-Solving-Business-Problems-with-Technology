## What Is OneDrive?

OneDrive is Microsoft's cloud storage and synchronization service. When you save a file to your OneDrive folder on your laptop, it is automatically copied to Microsoft's servers and made available on every other device connected to the same account.

This means:

- You can never truly "lose" the file to a dead laptop — it lives in the cloud.
- Every device stays current — edit on your phone and the changes appear on your laptop.
- Sharing is a link, not an attachment — colleagues can see the live version, never a stale copy.
- Version history is automatic — OneDrive saves prior versions so you can roll back mistakes.

> **Endicott-Specific Detail**
>
> Your Endicott College Microsoft account includes **1 TB of OneDrive storage**. This is separate from any personal Microsoft account you may already have. Always confirm you are signed in to your **@mail.endicott.edu** account — not a personal Hotmail or Outlook.com account — before saving course files.

## File Naming Convention

Every file you save/upload in BUS123 must follow the course naming convention. This makes it easy to identify, sort, and share your work without confusion.

**Format:** `LastName_FirstName_BUS123_ModuleN.xlsx`

**Example:** `Evitts_Bethany_BUS123_Module01.xlsx`

>
> I receive dozens of files with names like `homework.xlsx` or `final_FINAL_v3.xlsx`. A properly named file tells me exactly who submitted it and what it contains — before it is even opened. This is a professional habit that carries into every workplace. Get in that habit now.

## Saving, Syncing, and Verifying a File

Saving a file to your OneDrive folder is not the same as the file being available in the cloud. There is a brief delay between saving locally and the file completing its upload to Microsoft's servers. **Always verify sync status before submitting.**

| Sync Icon             | What It Means                        | What to Do                                      |
|-----------------------|--------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------|
| 🔵 Blue circle/arrows | File is currently uploading          | Wait — do not submit yet                        |
| ✅ Green checkmark    | File is fully synced to the cloud    | Safe to copy share link and submit              |
| ❌ Red X              | Sync error — file did not upload     | Check your internet connection and re-save      |

## Why Technology Matters in Business

In the first lessons of BUS123 you installed Microsoft 365, set up your OneDrive folder structure, and learned what this course is and how it works. You now have the tools and the expectations.

This lesson asks a sharper question: **why does any of it matter?** Before you learn how to build a payroll model or analyze inventory data, it helps to understand the larger context — how technology actually functions inside a business, and what separates someone who uses it casually from someone who uses it professionally.

---

## Passive User vs. Professional Operator

Most people interact with technology every day without thinking about it deliberately. A **passive user** opens an app, does a task, and closes it. A **professional operator** thinks about what output the tool needs to produce, how to structure the work so others can verify it, and how to catch errors before they matter.

| Dimension          | Passive User                                  | Professional Operator                                                              |
|--------------------|-----------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Goal**           | Complete the task                             | Produce reliable output someone else can use                                       |
| **File management**| Saves wherever is convenient                  | Follows a naming and folder convention every time                                  |
| **Formula use**    | Types answers directly into cells             | References input cells so results update automatically                             |
| **Error response** | Deletes the error and retypes a number        | Traces the formula to find and fix the root cause                                  |
| **Sharing**        | Emails an attachment                          | Shares a live OneDrive link to the current version                                 |

> ✅ **The Shift This Course Is Asking You to Make**
>
> You do not need to be an expert to be a professional operator. You need to be **deliberate**. The habits you build in BUS123 — naming files correctly, using formulas instead of typed values, checking your work before submitting — are the same habits that make a junior analyst trustworthy to a manager.

## How Technology Shapes Decisions

A spreadsheet does not just execute a calculation you already know how to do. It changes what questions you can even ask.

Consider a retail buyer deciding how many units to order. Without a model, the decision is a guess. With a properly built spreadsheet, the buyer can run ten scenarios in two minutes and identify the order quantity that minimizes risk under each one.

This is the core reason BUS123 exists: **technology does not replace business judgment — it amplifies it.** The analyst who can build the model gets to participate in the decision. The one who cannot has to wait for someone else's output.

## Where AI Fits In

AI tools, including tools like Microsoft Copilot, Claude, and ChatGPT in Excel and Word, are increasingly present in business workflows. It is worth being honest about what they do and do not change.

| AI Is Good At                                     | AI Is Not Good At                                                        |
|---------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Drafting a first version of something             | Knowing whether the answer is correct for your specific situation        |
| Summarizing large amounts of text                 | Catching errors in a formula it did not write                            |
| Suggesting functions you did not know existed     | Understanding the business context behind the numbers                    |
| Automating repetitive formatting tasks            | Taking responsibility for a wrong result                                 |

> ⚠️ **The Skill AI Cannot Replace**
>
> AI tools are most valuable to people who **already know how to do the work**. If you cannot read a formula and judge whether it is correct, you cannot evaluate what AI produces. BUS123 builds the foundation that makes AI useful rather than dangerous.

## Technology Habits That Pay Off

The research on early-career outcomes consistently shows that digital skill gaps are one of the most common reasons recent graduates underperform expectations. The habits below are simple — but most students do not practice them until they learn the hard way.

**Name every file correctly, every time.**
File naming is not a preference. It is a professional standard. An improperly named file signals carelessness before the content is even opened.

**Save to the cloud, not the device.**
Local saves are one hardware failure away from lost work. Cloud saves are versioned, backed up, and accessible from anywhere.

**Use formulas, not typed values.**
A typed number in a result cell is invisible to the next person who opens the file. A formula is a documented, auditable calculation.

**Check your output before you share it.**
Sending a file with a formula error is worse than sending it late. Spend two minutes reviewing before submitting anything.

**Learn the keyboard shortcut for what you do most.**
Speed compounds. Ten seconds saved per task adds up to hours across a semester — and across a career.
