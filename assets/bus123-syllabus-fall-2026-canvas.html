What this course is

An introductory lecture and applied workshop that prepares you with foundational technology and business-math skills for later School of Business courses and real-world work. You will explore current tools used in standard business operations and apply mathematics to accounting, finance, and management situations.

Prerequisites: None

Course details

Class type: Lecture / applied workshop

Format: Guided labs, case work, and Canvas-supported activities

Term: Fall 2026

Learning Outcomes

By the end of BUS123, you will be able to…

1

Organize and communicate. Use Microsoft 365, Windows, OneDrive, and Excel to manage business information.

2

Build accurate workbooks. Use Excel for business math, data visualization, and decision support.

3

Apply business math. Calculate percents, equations, pricing, payroll, depreciation, inventory, taxes, interest, time value, and basic statistics.

4

Choose the right workflow. Select appropriate technology for a business problem and explain your reasoning.

5

Work with integrity. Practice accuracy, transparency, responsible AI use, and truthful reporting.

Teaching and learning strategies

Short lectures, guided Excel labs, case-based workshops, pre-reading checks, and in-class problem solving using current business scenarios.

Know the guardrails

Course policies

Academic integrity

You are required to follow Endicott College's Academic Integrity Policy.

Responsible AI: use it, verify it, own it

Follow Endicott College's AI Policy and the Academic Integrity Policy; violations are subject to the college's Academic Integrity Policy. AI tools may be used for the following purposes when the instructor permits them. Unless an assignment gives different instructions, disclose meaningful AI assistance, verify AI-generated output, and remain responsible for the accuracy, transparency, and integrity of everything you submit.

May use when permitted

  • Brainstorming
  • Study support
  • Drafting questions
  • Troubleshooting concepts

You must

  • Disclose meaningful AI assistance
  • Verify AI-generated output
  • Explain and defend your work
  • Remain responsible for accuracy

Do not submit

  • Fabricated analysis
  • Uncited AI-written text
  • Work you cannot explain
  • AI use prohibited by the assignment
