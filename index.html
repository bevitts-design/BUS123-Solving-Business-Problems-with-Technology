01 · What changed

The crowding was structural. Here's the fix.

You said the slides felt cramped, the text felt small, and the cards stacked too densely — but also that there was a lot of blank space. Both at once is a sign the system was working against you, not for you. These four moves resolve it.

Before

Clamp-based sizing

  • Body type bottomed out around 14px on small viewports
  • Padding and gaps scaled too, so density got tighter on smaller screens
  • No fixed "authored" target — every layout was a guess

After

Fixed 1920×1080 + scaled stage

  • Author at one size; deck-stage.js letterbox-scales to any viewport
  • Body 28px minimum, titles 72px — always projectable
  • One Tweaks slider rescales the whole deck for back-of-room or laptop

Before

Four-font stack

  • Cinzel + Cormorant Garamond + DM Mono + Inter
  • Cinzel is all-caps display — unreadable at small sizes
  • Two serifs competing for the display role

After

Three-font stack

  • Instrument Serif for display — warm, modern, reads down to 30px
  • Geist for body/UI — geometric, clean, excellent at slide sizes
  • JetBrains Mono for formulas, Excel, eyebrows

Before

Dense card layouts

  • Up to 7 chunks per slide, often 5+ accent-stripe cards
  • Header bars, gradient bars, slide numbers, badges — all stacking
  • No room for images, diagrams, or motion

After

Image-led, 3-chunk max

  • Every content template offers an <image-slot> placeholder
  • Motion-graphic placeholders for animated diagrams
  • Cards lose the left-stripe; bullets become numbered statements

Before

Hand-rolled navigation

  • Per-deck keyboard JS, slide counter, staggered reveals
  • No print path, no thumbnail rail, no speaker-notes contract
  • Every deck reimplements the same boilerplate

After

<deck-stage> component

  • Keyboard nav, tap regions, slide counter, thumbnail rail — all free
  • Speaker-notes JSON wired up automatically
  • Print-to-PDF: Ctrl/Cmd-P, one slide per page
