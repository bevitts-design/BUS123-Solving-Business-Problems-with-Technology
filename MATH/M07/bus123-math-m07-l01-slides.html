Part 1 of 3
Sales Tax
The merchant as collection agent
Sales Tax · Concept
The Merchant Is a Collection Agent
Consumer Perspective
You Pay It
The consumer sees a line item at checkout. They pay it and forget about it.
Business Perspective
You Hold It — Temporarily
The merchant collects tax funds from the customer and holds them in trust for the government. It is a liability, not revenue. Misclassifying collected tax as earned revenue is a compliance and cash-flow error.
Sales Tax · Tidal Goods Co.
Order of Operations
1
Subtract trade discount from retail price first
Taxable = Price − Trade Discount
2
Apply tax rate to the taxable subtotal
Tax = Taxable × Rate
3
Add shipping after tax has been calculated
Total = Taxable + Tax + Shipping
// Tidal Goods Co. — wholesale order

Retail price: $480.00
Trade discount: − $80.00
──────────────────────────
Taxable subtotal: $400.00
× 6% tax: × 0.06
Tax owed: $24.00
──────────────────────────
Subtotal + Tax: $424.00
+ Shipping: + $15.00
Total Price: $439.00
Sales Tax · Excel
Building the Formula Chain
// Excel structure — Tidal Goods Co.

B1 = 480 // Retail price
B2 = 80 // Trade discount
B3 = 0.06 // Tax rate
B4 = 15 // Shipping

B5 = =B1-B2 // Taxable subtotal
B6 = =B5*B3 // Tax owed
B7 = =B5+B6+B4 // Total price
Key Insight
When inputs are in separate cells, changing any one value — discount rate, shipping, tax rate — recalculates the entire chain automatically. This is the Excel advantage over a one-time manual calculation.
Sales Tax · Reverse Calculation
What Were the Actual Sales?

Tidal Goods Co.'s register shows $52,000 in total receipts for the day. The store collects 6.5% sales tax. What was the actual pre-tax revenue?

// Formula

Actual Sales = Total Sales ÷ (1 + Tax Rate)

=B1/(1+B2)

= $52,000 ÷ 1.065
= $48,826.29
Why This Matters
$52,000 is NOT revenue. Of that total, $3,173.71 belongs to the state. Reporting $52,000 as revenue overstates earnings by over 6%.

Excise Tax
Baked in before the customer sees the price
Excise Tax · Concept
Upstream, Embedded, Invisible to Consumers
What It Is
A targeted tax on specific goods — fuel, alcohol, tobacco, luxury appliances. Imposed on manufacturers or wholesalers, not at the retail register.
Where It's Paid
Upstream — before the product reaches a retailer. By the time a consumer sees the sticker price, excise tax is already included.
Pricing Strategy Impact
Demand Elasticity Risk
Because excise tax raises your inventory acquisition cost, you must raise retail prices to maintain margin. The risk: do your customers walk when the price goes up? This is the elasticity question.
Fuel Alcohol Tobacco Luxury appliances Firearms
Excise Tax · Meridian Advisory Group
When Both Taxes Apply — Never Compound Them
// Meridian Advisory — commercial appliance
// Base cost: $6,200 | Sales tax: 7% | Excise: 8%

Sales Tax = $6,200 × 0.07 = $434.00
Excise Tax = $6,200 × 0.08 = $496.00
──────────────────────────────────────
Total Cost = $6,200 + $434 + $496 = $7,130

// Shortcut: (7% + 8%) = 15% combined rate
= $6,200 × 1.15 = $7,130
Critical Rule
Both taxes hit the BASE
Do NOT apply sales tax to the excise-tax-included amount. Both percentages are calculated on the original $6,200 separately. Adding the rates first and multiplying once gives the same correct answer.
Excise Tax · Excel
Two Approaches, One Answer
// Approach 1 — Separate calculations

B1 = 6200 // Base cost
B2 = 0.07 // Sales tax rate
B3 = 0.08 // Excise tax rate

B4 = =B1*B2 // Sales tax amt
B5 = =B1*B3 // Excise tax amt
B6 = =B1+B4+B5 // Total cost
// Approach 2 — Combined rate shortcut

B1 = 6200 // Base cost
B2 = 0.07 // Sales tax rate
B3 = 0.08 // Excise tax rate

B4 = =B1*(1+B2+B3)

Both return $7,130 ✓
Part 3 of 3
Property Tax
Three steps · four decimal places · every dollar of assessed value
Property Tax · Concept
The Asset-Owner's Obligation
What It Funds
Local schools, fire & police protection, roads, infrastructure. Property tax is the primary municipal revenue source.
What's Taxable
Land Buildings Machinery Computers Furniture
Triple Net Lease Alert
NNN Leases Pass Tax to the Tenant
In a Triple Net (NNN) lease, the tenant — not the landlord — pays property taxes, insurance, and maintenance. A business that signs a NNN lease without researching local property tax rates is signing a blank check on their overhead.
Property Tax · Protocol
Three Steps, Always in This Order
1
Assessed Value — The municipality taxes only a fraction of market value
Assessed Value = Assessment Rate × Market Value
2
Tax Rate — Determined by the town's budget need divided by total assessed value
Round to the nearest ten-thousandth (4 decimal places)
Tax Rate = Town Budget ÷ Total Assessed Value
3
Annual Tax Due — Apply the rounded rate to your assessed value
Annual Tax = Tax Rate × Assessed Value
Property Tax · Meridian Advisory Group
Full Three-Step Walkthrough
Given
Market Value of Office: $820,000
Assessment Rate: 40%
Town Budget: $310,000
Total Assessed Value in Town: $4,750,000
// Step 1 — Assessed Value
= 0.40 × $820,000 → $328,000

// Step 2 — Tax Rate (round to 4 decimals)
= $310,000 ÷ $4,750,000 = 0.065263...
Rounded: → 0.0653 (6.53%)

// Step 3 — Annual Tax Due
= 0.0653 × $328,000 → $21,418.40
Watch Out
Two Mistakes That Show Up Every Exam
⚠ Mistake 1 · Sales Tax
Applying Tax Before the Trade Discount
Wrong: $480 × 0.06 = $28.80 tax, then subtract $80

Right: $480 − $80 = $400 taxable, then $400 × 0.06 = $24.00

Taxing the full retail price before the discount inflates the tax — and the customer's total.
⚠ Mistake 2 · Excise Tax
Compounding the Two Tax Rates
Wrong: Apply 7% to base, then apply 8% to the tax-included amount

Right: Both 7% and 8% are calculated on the original base price separately

Compounding overstates total cost and corrupts your margin analysis.

Key Takeaways
What to Carry Forward
01
Sales Tax: Order Matters
Trade discount first, then tax, then shipping. Merchants collect — they don't own the funds. Reverse-calculate actual sales with Total ÷ (1 + Rate).
02
Excise Tax: Both Hit the Base
Excise tax is baked in upstream. When both excise and sales tax apply, calculate each from the original cost separately — never compound them.
03
Property Tax: Three Steps, Four Decimals
Assessed value → tax rate (rounded to 4 decimal places) → annual tax due. NNN leases pass all three to the tenant — research the municipality before signing.
