BUS123 Example Capstone Project

Northstar Athletic Studio Finance Decision

Build an Excel model from a data packet, format it professionally, compare financing options, and recommend the launch plan most likely to keep a new business alive in its first year.

Open Starter Workbook

Business Scenario

Northstar Athletic Studio is a new indoor cycling studio preparing to open near a college town. The founder needs financing for bikes, sound equipment, renovation, booking software, launch marketing, and an initial cash cushion.

Which financing option gives Northstar Athletic Studio the best chance of surviving its first year?

Your recommendation should use numbers from your workbook and explain the tradeoff between monthly cash flow, ownership, fixed payments, and membership growth assumptions. The starter workbook gives you the case data, but you are responsible for creating the model tabs, row labels, column headers, formulas, formatting, charts, dashboard, and recommendation section.

You may use approved course resources, Excel help, and your own notes, but you must be able to explain every formula, formatting choice, chart, and recommendation in your workbook. Work that cannot be explained may lose credit even if the final workbook appears complete.

Workbook Build Requirements

Your starter workbook includes only START HERE and Assumptions. You must create the remaining tabs, rows, columns, formulas, charts, and formatting yourself.

  1. Startup Budget: create a table, organize launch costs, link costs from assumptions, and confirm total funding needed.
  2. Financing Model: create a financing comparison table, calculate debt payments using PMT, and model investor funding as a profit share.
  3. Revenue Forecast: build 12-month revenue tables for conservative, expected, and optimistic scenarios.
  4. Cash Flow Model: create a first-year cash flow comparison for all three financing options.
  5. Break-Even Analysis: estimate the members needed to cover monthly costs and financing obligations.
  6. Dashboard: design a clear summary with key metrics, charts, and a recommendation.
  7. Recommendation: write a short evidence-backed business recommendation.

Construction Instructions

1. Create Your Tabs

Create new worksheet tabs in a logical order. A strong order is: START HERE, Assumptions, Startup Budget, Financing Model, Revenue Forecast, Cash Flow Model, Break-Even Analysis, Dashboard, Recommendation.

2. Build the Startup Budget

Create a clean table with startup cost item and cost columns. Link the cost values from the Assumptions tab instead of retyping them. Add a total row for total funding needed.

  • Use a bold title at the top.
  • Format dollar amounts as currency.
  • Use a distinct fill color for the table header.
  • Use a border or shading to make the total row stand out.

3. Build the Financing Model

Create a table comparing the bank loan, equipment financing, and investor funding options. Include amount financed, APR, term, monthly fixed payment, profit-share percentage, and notes about risk or ownership.

Use Excel's PMT function for the bank loan and equipment financing payments. APR is annual, but payments are monthly, so divide APR by 12 and multiply years by 12.

Course skill reminder:
  • PMT was covered in the time value of money lesson. Use the same monthly rate and monthly period logic here.
  • Break-even was covered in the pricing lesson. Use contribution logic: what amount is available each month to cover the costs that still need to be paid?
  • For the investor payment, remember the same guardrail logic used in overtime formulas: use a function such as MAX when a value should not drop below zero. Your job is to adapt that idea to positive monthly operating profit without typing the calculated answer manually.

4. Build the Revenue Forecast

Create a 12-month forecast. For each month, calculate membership revenue, drop-in revenue, private event revenue, and total revenue. Use the membership counts and revenue assumptions from the Assumptions tab.

5. Build the Cash Flow Model

Create a 12-month expected-scenario cash flow comparison for all three financing options. Calculate expected revenue, operating costs, financing costs, and monthly cash flow.

The investor receives 18% of positive monthly operating profit. If operating profit is negative, the investor payment should be $0. Use a formula such as MAX to prevent negative investor payments.

6. Build the Break-Even Analysis

Estimate the number of members needed each month to cover operating costs and financing obligations under each option. Round member counts appropriately and add a short interpretation.

7. Build the Dashboard

Design a dashboard with monthly fixed financing cost, lowest monthly cash flow, total first-year cash flow, break-even members, at least one financing comparison chart, at least one cash-flow-over-time chart, and a clear recommended option.

8. Build the Recommendation Section

Write a short recommendation that names the option you recommend, explains why the numbers support it, identifies the risk that could change your answer, and discusses the tradeoff between cash, control, and flexibility.

Recommendation Prompt

Based on your model, which financing option gives Northstar Athletic Studio the best chance of surviving its first year?

Your answer should use numbers from your workbook and explain the tradeoff between risk, ownership, monthly cash flow, and growth assumptions.

Process Evidence

Because this project assesses how you build a spreadsheet model, you must submit evidence of your process in addition to the finished workbook.

Build Log

Create a Build Log section in your Recommendation tab or add a separate Build Log tab. Write 5-8 short bullets explaining how you built the workbook.

  • Which tabs you created first and why.
  • One formatting choice that made the workbook easier to read.
  • One formula or model section you had to revise.
  • One check you used to make sure the model was reasonable.
  • One decision you made about the dashboard layout.

Formula Explanations

Explain at least four formulas in plain English: bank loan monthly payment, equipment financing monthly payment, investor payment, and break-even members.

In-Class Checkpoint

During class, be prepared to show one partially built section of your workbook, such as your startup budget table, financing model table, cash flow model skeleton, or first dashboard layout draft.

Version Evidence

If you work in OneDrive or Excel online, keep your version history available. Your instructor may ask you to show how the workbook developed over time.

Short Defense

Be ready for a 2-3 minute explanation of your work. You may be asked to walk through your cash flow model, explain the investor payment formula, defend your recommendation, update an assumption, or explain a formatting choice.

Individual Variation

Your instructor may give you or your group one changed assumption, such as a different APR, rent amount, investor percentage, or membership forecast. If assigned, update your model and explain whether your recommendation changes.
