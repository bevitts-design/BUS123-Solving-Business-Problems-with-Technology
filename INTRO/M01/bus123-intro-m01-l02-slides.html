Warm-Up: What Is a Business Problem?

02

Every business — from a surf shop to a hospital — faces problems that require data, analysis, and decisions.

  • A retailer needs to know which products are selling and which are sitting on shelves
  • A payroll manager needs to calculate 50 employees' net pay — accurately, every two weeks
  • A small event company needs to know how many events it must book just to break even
  • A medical center needs to analyze patient data to understand which insurance plans drive revenue
  • What do all of these have in common? They can all be solved with Excel.
💡

This course is not about memorizing formulas. It is about developing the habit of turning a business question into a spreadsheet answer.

What Is This Course?

04

BUS123 is a hands-on, tool-based course. Every class period you will be building something — a spreadsheet, a model, a calculation — using real business scenarios.

Track 1Introduction — Office 365, OneDrive, digital fluency
Track 2Excel Skills — formulas, functions, data, charts
Track 3Business Math — payroll, discounts, markup, interest, taxes
No Textbook
All materials built from scratch

There is no textbook for this course. Every pre-reading, activity, and workbook is created specifically for BUS123. All files are on GitHub and Canvas — free, always accessible.

🎯

The goal is simple: leave this course able to open Excel and solve any business math problem your employer puts in front of you.

Why Case Studies?

07

Abstract math is hard to care about. Real business scenarios make the math matter.

  • When you calculate payroll for Meridian Advisory Group, you are doing exactly what a payroll manager does every two weeks
  • When you build a breakeven model for Anchor & Oak Events, you are making the same calculation a business owner makes before signing a lease
  • When you analyze inventory for Tidal Goods Co., you are doing what a retail buyer does every quarter
  • When you interpret payer mix data for Harborside Medical Center, you are doing what a healthcare CFO reviews every month
The Point

Every number you calculate in this course has a real-world analog. You are not doing homework — you are practicing skills that professionals use in actual jobs.

Common Mistake

11
⚠️SUBMITTING THE WRONG FILE OR VERSION

Canvas records your submission timestamp and file name. Uploading the wrong version — or uploading a blank file — counts as your submission.

Common Error

Uploading a blank starter file, an old version from a previous assignment, or a file named "Untitled" — then asking for a re-submission opportunity.

Best Practice

Open your submitted file after uploading to confirm it is the correct version with all your work completed. Takes 30 seconds and prevents every submission error.

Standards-Based Learning

14

This course uses a 4-point proficiency scale rather than traditional percentage scoring.

4Exceeds — demonstrates mastery beyond expectations, applies concepts in new contexts
3Meets — grade-level expectation, concepts applied correctly
2Approaching — partial understanding, some errors present
1Beginning — limited understanding, significant gaps remain
The Goal
Level 3 = success

A score of 3 (Meets) means you have mastered the standard at the level expected. Level 4 requires you to apply skills in unfamiliar contexts — that's the challenge tab in your workbooks.

📋

There are 20 standards across 7 skill strands this semester — Technology, Excel, Math, Merchandising, Operating Costs, Finance, and Data Analysis.

Your Workbook Structure

15

Every Excel workbook in this course follows the same tab structure.

Tab 1: START HERE  —  instructions, learning objectives, how to use the workbook
Tab 2: Main Activity  —  primary exercise (yellow cells = your input)
Tab 3: Challenge    —  Level 4 stretch exercise (optional but encouraged)
Tab 4: Formula Reference Card  —  every formula from this lesson
Yellow Cells
Your input goes here — always

Yellow-filled cells are where you type your answers. Never type in cells that are not yellow unless instructed.

Formula First Rule
Figure out the function yourself

Workbooks never tell you which Excel function to use. Read the context, use the Formula Reference Card, and choose the right tool — like a real analyst would.

AI Tools & Academic Integrity

18

AI tools like ChatGPT and Claude are part of the modern business toolkit. This course takes a practical approach.

Permitted
  • Using AI to understand a concept you're stuck on
  • Asking AI to explain an Excel error message
  • Using AI to check your work after completing it
  • Research and background reading on business topics
Not Permitted

  • Submitting AI-generated answers as your own calculations
  • Using AI during Canvas quizzes
  • Copying AI output without understanding it
🧠

The test is simple: can you explain what you submitted? If a professor asks you to walk through your work and you cannot — that is an academic integrity issue.

Common Mistake

19
⚠️TYPING THE ANSWER INSTEAD OF BUILDING THE FORMULA

A yellow cell that says $1,247.50 might look correct. But if you typed that number instead of writing a formula, your workbook is broken.

Wrong Approach

Calculate the answer on paper or in your head → type the final number into the cell. This looks right but earns no credit for formula construction.

Correct Approach

Type = in the cell → build the formula using cell references and functions → Excel calculates the answer. If input values change, your answer updates automatically.
